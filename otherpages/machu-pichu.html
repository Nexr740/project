Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu is an ancient Inca citadel located high in the Andes Mountains of Peru, at an altitude of about 2,430 meters (7,970 feet) above sea level. It was built in the 15th century during the reign of Inca emperor Pachacuti, and its purpose remains a subject of speculationâ€”though it is believed to have been a royal estate or religious site. The site was abandoned in the 16th century during the Spanish Conquest and remained largely forgotten by the outside world until its rediscovery by American historian Hiram Bingham in 1911.Machu Picchu is renowned for its advanced dry-stone construction, precise stonework, and sophisticated agricultural terraces. It is divided into two main areas: the agricultural sector with terraced fields and the urban sector, which includes temples, plazas, and residential buildings. The most famous structure within the site is the **Intihuatana** stone, often called the "hitching post of the sun," which is believed to have been used for astronomical purposes.Today, Machu Picchu is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the most visited tourist attractions in the world. It is accessible by a combination of train, bus, and hiking, including the renowned Inca Trail. The site remains a symbol of the Inca Empire and is a testament to its remarkable engineering and cultural achievements.
