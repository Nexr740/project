Chichén Itzá

Chichén Itzá is an ancient Mayan city located in the Yucatán Peninsula of Mexico, and one of the most famous archaeological sites of the Mayan civilization. It was a major political, economic, and cultural center from the 7th to the 10th century AD, before it was abandoned.The site is renowned for its impressive structures, most notably the **Pyramid of Kukulcán** (also called El Castillo), a step pyramid dedicated to the serpent god Kukulcán. The pyramid is aligned with astronomical events, and during the spring and autumn equinoxes, the play of light and shadow creates the illusion of a serpent slithering down its steps. Other notable structures include the **Temple of the Warriors**, the **Ball Court** (where the Mesoamerican ballgame was played), and the **Observatory** (El Caracol), which was used for astronomical observations.Chichén Itzá was a major urban hub and a center for Mayan ritual and governance, with advanced knowledge of architecture, astronomy, and mathematics. In 1988, it was designated a UNESCO World Heritage site and in 2007, it was named one of the **New Seven Wonders of the World.
